About Jiu-Jitsu

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is a grappling martial art, like wrestling or judo – it involves no striking.

Jiu-jitsu focuses on controlling an opponent’s movement and the application of fight-ending strangles and joint-locks.

The sport gained popularity after establishing itself as the foundational grappling art in MMA, but is also trained for fitness, self-defense, and recreation.

Jiu-jitsu is a sport both broad and deep, practiced by millions of men, women, and children around the world.

About Bold Spirit

Bold Spirit is a Brazilian jiu-jitsu academy founded in 2023. We’re located in Oak Harbor on Whidbey Island.

Every gym has its own way of doing things. At Bold Spirit, our way of doing things emphasizes:

  • Simplicity. Jiu-jitsu is a complex sport, but we find the core ideas and principles that apply again and again.
  • Control. Across the thousands of configurations two opponents can be in, the north star for grappling is control of your opponent’s movement. Of course, being focused on this is a type of self-control, which we also emphasize.
  • A better life. It sounds cliché, but it’s obvious to longtime practitioners: jiu-jitsu is a way to live better.

Adults - $120/month

  • For ages 12+
  • Men and women
  • Beginner to advanced
  • Classes in both gi (traditional kimono) and no-gi (e.g. rash guard, board shorts, and spats)

We cover a wide variety of techniques and drills, from beginner to advanced, for men and women.

Furthermore, the adults classes are broken down into gi and no-gi classes. The gi is the traditional Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu attire, but no-gi training (in rash guard, board shorts, and spats) is a popular version of the sport as well.

Students are encouraged to train both gi and no-gi. Neither is a prerequisite for the other.

Women Only - $40/month

  • Female instructor, all-women class
  • Same skills as co-ed Adults class, but with greater emphasis on practical self-defense

In addition to the co-ed Adults class, we have a women-only adult class. We cover the same techniques and skills as the co-ed adult classes, but with a greater emphasis on practical self-defense.

The women’s class is taught by a female instructor, and open to ages 10+.
